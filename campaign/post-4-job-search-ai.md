# Post 4: "I built an AI system to manage my job search. Here's what it taught me."
**Publish: Fri Apr 18** -- Meta, relatable, shows full-stack AI thinking

---

I'm in a job search right now. Like a lot of senior engineering leaders.

But instead of spreadsheets and sticky notes, I built an AI-powered system to manage the whole thing. Because that's apparently how my brain works.

Here's what it does:

It scores every job description against my actual experience. Not keyword matching like an ATS. Real semantic analysis of whether my 20 years of building healthcare platforms, scaling teams, and shipping AI products maps to what they're asking for.

Then it tailors my resume for each role. Not a template swap. It extracts the exact keywords from the JD, checks whether each one appears literally in the resume (because that's how ATS systems actually work), and shows me what's missing before I hit submit.

The ATS readiness score was the breakthrough. I was getting auto-rejected from roles I was clearly qualified for. Turns out Workday doesn't care that you "architected cloud solutions" if the JD says "AWS" and that exact word isn't in your resume. Literal. String. Matching.

Since adding the ATS checker, my callback rate has improved noticeably.

The interesting part isn't the tool. It's the insight: most companies are filtering candidates through systems that can't read. And most candidates are writing resumes for humans who never see them.

If you're in a job search, the single most impactful thing you can do is make sure the exact words from the job posting appear in your resume. Not synonyms. The actual words.
