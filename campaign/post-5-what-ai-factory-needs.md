# Post 5: "Most companies building an 'AI Factory' are missing the factory part."
**Publish: Mon Apr 21** -- Thought leadership, targets VP AI roles directly

---

I keep seeing job postings for "VP of AI" and "Head of AI Factory" at large enterprises. The descriptions are ambitious. The requirements lists are long.

But most of them are missing the point.

They focus on the AI. They should focus on the factory.

An AI Factory isn't a team of data scientists running notebooks. It's an engineering operation. And operations need:

1. Repeatable processes. Your first model can be artisanal. Your 50th cannot. You need pipelines that extract, train, validate, deploy, and monitor without heroics.

2. Governance from day one. Not bolted on after legal gets nervous. Data lineage, bias detection, access controls, and audit trails built into the platform, not documented in a wiki nobody reads.

3. Cross-functional integration. The best model in the world is worthless if the business can't use it. The hard work isn't training. It's deploying into existing workflows, measuring ROI, and iterating based on what the operations team actually sees.

4. A builder who's done it before. AI strategy decks don't ship products. You need someone who's scaled engineering teams, integrated with messy enterprise systems, and shipped under compliance constraints.

I've spent 20 years doing exactly that in healthcare: building teams from zero, connecting 18+ EHR systems, shipping AI into clinical workflows under HIPAA, and measuring outcomes. The domain was healthcare. The discipline is the same one an AI Factory needs.

If your organization is standing up an AI Factory, the first hire shouldn't be a researcher. It should be an engineering leader who knows how to build the factory floor.
