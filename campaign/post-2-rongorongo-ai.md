# Post 2: "I'm using AI to decipher a script nobody can read."
**Publish: Mon Apr 14** -- Shows genuine AI passion, memorable, humanizing

---

I'm using AI to decipher a script nobody can read.

Rongorongo is an undeciphered writing system from Easter Island. Fewer than 30 artifacts exist. No Rosetta Stone. No bilingual texts. Linguists have been stuck for 150 years.

I thought: what if modern NLP could see patterns humans can't?

So I started building:
- Digitized the glyph corpus from academic sources
- Fine-tuned multilingual LLMs on Polynesian language families
- Built statistical models to detect recurring sequences and potential syntax
- Used computer vision to catalog and cluster individual glyphs

Has it worked? Honestly, not yet. The corpus is tiny and the problem is genuinely hard. But the process has taught me more about NLP, model fine-tuning, and working with sparse data than any corporate project ever could.

Here's what I think people get wrong about AI leadership: they think it means managing a team that builds AI. It doesn't. It means understanding what the models can and can't do, where they break, and what it takes to make them useful on hard problems.

You get that by building. Not by reading whitepapers.

I run my Easter Island research the same way I run engineering orgs: hypothesis, experiment, measure, iterate. The only difference is the dataset is 600 years old.
