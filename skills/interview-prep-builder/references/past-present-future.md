# Past, Present, Future — Interview Opener Framework

The default opening for every interview. When asked "Tell me about yourself" or
any variant, deliver this structured 60-90 second intro. Everyone loves this format
because it tells a complete story with clear direction.

## Structure

### Past (Track record + proof point)
- Your career arc in one sentence
- Your biggest accomplishment with a specific number
- Establishes credibility immediately

### Present (What you're doing now + unique edge)
- What you're actively working on or learning
- What makes you different from other candidates at your level
- Shows you're not coasting — you're building

### Future (Why THIS company + alignment)
- Reference something specific about the target company or role
- Connect their mission/challenge to your experience
- Show you've done your homework and this isn't a generic pitch

## Rules

1. Each section: 2-3 sentences max
2. Must include at least one hard number (Past section)
3. Future must reference something specific about the target company — never generic
4. Total delivery: 60-90 seconds spoken aloud
5. Past and Present are semi-stable across interviews; Future is fully customized each time
6. Practice out loud — this is a spoken format, not a written essay

## Proven Example (Orchestration Works — CTO role)

> **Past:** "I've spent the last 20 years in healthcare software — building teams
> from zero and shipping production systems. I've built three engineering orgs from
> scratch and scaled a fourth from 4 to 25. Most recently as CTO at a value-based
> care organization, I built a protocol-agnostic integration engine connecting to
> 18+ hospital systems — Epic, Athena, CMS — via FHIR and HL7 on Azure, with
> bi-directional data flow. We expanded beneficiary coverage 55% while maintaining
> 99.9% uptime."
>
> **Present:** "Right now I'm actively building with AI coding agents every day —
> I believe if you're not running out of tokens, you're not thinking big enough.
> I'm also bridging the gap between technical and business — I started as a coder,
> went back and got an MBA specifically to speak both languages."
>
> **Future:** "What excites me about Orchestration Works is you're taking that same
> intersection I've lived in — healthcare data, AI, and automation — and pointing
> it at A/R follow-up with real autonomy to build it right. The deterministic
> foundation plus AI enrichment approach is exactly how I've architected systems.
> I want to be where the action is, building something that matters."

## Customization Guide

When generating PPF for a new interview:

- **Past:** Pull from `master/achievements.md`. Lead with years of experience in
  the relevant domain. Choose the achievement that most closely matches the target
  role's primary requirement. Always include a number.
- **Present:** Highlight current activities that show growth and relevance. The AI
  coding agents angle is strong for tech-forward companies. The MBA bridge is strong
  for roles requiring business acumen. Pick what resonates with the specific role.
- **Future:** Read the job description and company-brief.md carefully. Find the
  intersection between what they're building and what you've done. Name the company.
  Name their specific challenge or approach. Make it clear this isn't a form letter.
