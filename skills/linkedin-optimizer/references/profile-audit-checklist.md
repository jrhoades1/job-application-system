# LinkedIn Profile Audit Checklist

> Reference for the linkedin-optimizer skill. Covers every profile section
> that affects job recommendation quality, with specific criteria for
> Director/VP-level engineering leaders.

## Headline (220 characters max)

- [ ] Contains target job title verbatim (e.g., "Director of Engineering")
- [ ] Includes 1-2 semantic peer titles or keywords (e.g., "AI/ML", "Healthcare IT")
- [ ] Has at least one quantified differentiator (e.g., "Scaling Teams 4→25")
- [ ] No filler words ("passionate," "results-driven," "thought leader")
- [ ] Uses all available characters (longer headlines = more keyword surface area)

**Template:** `[Target Title] | [Domain Expertise] | [Differentiator with Number] | [Second Differentiator]`

**Example:** `Director of Engineering | Healthcare AI/ML | Built Teams 0→22 | FHIR/HL7 Integration at Scale`

## About Section (2,600 characters max)

### First 275 characters (mobile-visible hook)
- [ ] Opens with target title or equivalent positioning statement
- [ ] Includes 2-3 quantified achievements in the first two sentences
- [ ] No "I am a..." opening — lead with what you've done
- [ ] Contains at least one industry keyword (healthcare, fintech, etc.)

### Full About section
- [ ] Covers all differentiators from narrative.md
- [ ] Each claim has a number attached (team size, revenue, scale)
- [ ] Mentions specific technologies that appear in target job descriptions
- [ ] Closes with what you're looking for (title, domain, type of challenge)
- [ ] No more than 3-4 paragraphs — dense and scannable

## Experience Section

### For each role:
- [ ] Title matches or exceeds LinkedIn's seniority inference expectations
- [ ] Company name is correct and linked to the company page
- [ ] Date ranges are accurate (gaps will lower profile trust signals)
- [ ] 3-5 bullets per role, each in C-A-R format (Challenge → Action → Result)
- [ ] First bullet is the strongest accomplishment for that role
- [ ] Bullets contain keywords from target job descriptions
- [ ] Numbers appear in at least 60% of bullets (team size, %, $, scale)
- [ ] No responsibility lists without outcomes ("managed team" → "built team from 0→22, shipping 4 products annually")

### Title keywords that drive seniority inference:
- **Director level:** Director, Senior Director, Head of
- **VP level:** Vice President, VP, SVP
- **C-level:** CTO, CIO, Chief
- **Avoid:** Lead (ambiguous), Manager (may infer mid-level), Principal (IC signal)

## Skills Section (50 max)

### Cluster strategy (order matters — top 3 are pinned):
1. **Pin top 3:** Most relevant to target role + most endorsed
2. **Leadership cluster (8-10):** Engineering Leadership, Strategic Planning, Organizational Design, Cross-functional Collaboration, Headcount Planning, Executive Communication, Agile Transformation, Technical Strategy, Team Building, Talent Development
3. **Technical cluster (8-10):** System Architecture, Cloud Infrastructure (AWS/Azure), Microservices, CI/CD, DevOps, API Design, Data Engineering, AI/ML Integration, Performance Optimization, Platform Engineering
4. **Domain cluster (8-10):** Healthcare IT, HIPAA Compliance, FHIR, HL7, EHR Integration, Value-Based Care, Clinical Workflows, SaaS, SOC 2, HITRUST

### Skills to avoid (dilute Director-level signal):
- Individual contributor skills without leadership context (e.g., "CSS", "jQuery", "HTML")
- Generic skills (e.g., "Microsoft Office", "Communication")
- Outdated technologies not in target job descriptions
- Skills you can't speak to in an interview

## Education

- [ ] Degrees listed with correct institutions and years
- [ ] Relevant certifications added (AWS, PMP, etc.)
- [ ] No high school (unless only education)

## Recommendations

- [ ] At least 3 recommendations visible
- [ ] At least 1 from a direct report (shows leadership)
- [ ] At least 1 from a peer or cross-functional partner
- [ ] At least 1 from a manager or executive
- [ ] Recommendations mention specific qualities matching target roles

## Featured Section

- [ ] 2-3 items that showcase expertise
- [ ] At least one demonstrates thought leadership (post, article, talk)
- [ ] At least one shows technical depth (project, architecture doc, open source)
- [ ] No stale items (older than 2 years unless evergreen)

## Open to Work Settings

- [ ] Enabled with **Recruiters Only** visibility
- [ ] Exactly 5 job titles matching real job postings
- [ ] Locations set (including "Remote" explicitly if desired)
- [ ] Job type = Full-time (only add Contract if genuinely open)
- [ ] Start date reflects actual availability

## Profile Settings (Settings & Privacy → Job Seeking)

- [ ] "Use your data to recommend jobs" = ON
- [ ] "Share resume data with hirers" = ON
- [ ] "Signal your interest to recruiters at companies you've created job alerts for" = ON
- [ ] "Share with job poster" = ON

## Profile Photo & Banner

- [ ] Professional headshot (faces get 14x more profile views)
- [ ] Background banner is not default — shows industry or personal brand
- [ ] Profile URL is customized (linkedin.com/in/firstname-lastname)

## Activity & Engagement

- [ ] At least 1 post per month in target domain
- [ ] Engaging with content from target companies (like, comment)
- [ ] Following 5-10 target companies
- [ ] Connected with recruiters at target companies
- [ ] Not clicking into irrelevant job postings (negative signal training)
