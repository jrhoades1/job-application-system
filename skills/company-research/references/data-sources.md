# Company Research Data Sources

Checklist of what to research and where. Work through tiers in order — Tier 1 is
mandatory, Tier 2 fills gaps, Tier 3 applies only to tech-forward companies.

## Tier 1 — Must Research

### Job Description (Primary Signal)
The JD reveals more than most candidates realize:
- **Team structure:** Reporting lines, team size, cross-functional partners
- **Tech stack:** Explicit technologies and implied architecture patterns
- **Growth stage:** "Build from scratch" vs "scale existing" vs "maintain and optimize"
- **Pain points:** Requirements with urgency language ("immediately," "critical need")
- **Culture:** Formal vs casual tone, values emphasis, perks listed

Extract: team size, tech stack, growth indicators, urgency signals, culture markers.

### Company Website
- **About page:** Mission, founding story, leadership bios
- **Product pages:** What they build, who they serve, how they describe their value
- **Careers page:** Other open roles (reveals team gaps and growth areas)
- **Blog/newsroom:** Recent announcements, product launches, partnerships

Extract: mission, products, leadership names/titles, headcount signals, recent announcements.

### Recent News (Last 6 Months)
Search: "[Company name] news", "[Company name] funding", "[Company name] acquisition"
- Funding rounds (amount, investors, what they plan to use it for)
- Acquisitions (what capabilities they are buying)
- Product launches (what they are building)
- Leadership changes (new CEO/CTO signals strategy shift)
- Layoffs or restructuring (proceed with caution, not necessarily a dealbreaker)

Extract: timeline of events, funding total, strategic direction signals.

## Tier 2 — Research If Available

### Glassdoor / Indeed
Search: "[Company name] Glassdoor reviews"
- Overall rating (context: 3.5+ is decent, 4.0+ is good)
- **Pros patterns** — what multiple reviewers agree is good
- **Cons patterns** — what multiple reviewers flag (ignore one-off complaints)
- Interview process reviews — what to expect, difficulty level, timeline
- CEO approval rating

Extract: culture themes, interview process, consistent pros/cons across 5+ reviews.

### LinkedIn
Search: "[Company name] LinkedIn"
- Company size and growth trajectory (compare to 6 months / 1 year ago)
- Recent hires (who they are bringing in, what seniority)
- Key leaders — background, tenure, previous companies
- Hiring manager profile (if name is known from the JD or correspondence)

Extract: headcount, growth rate, leadership backgrounds, hiring manager intel.

### Crunchbase / PitchBook
Search: "[Company name] Crunchbase"
- Funding history (rounds, amounts, investors)
- Valuation (if available)
- Competitors listed
- Key people

Extract: total funding, last round date/amount, investor names, competitor list.

## Tier 3 — Tech Companies Only

### GitHub Organization
Search: "github.com/[company-name]"
- Open source repos — what they publish reveals stack and priorities
- Activity level — active commits vs abandoned repos
- Stars/forks — community engagement
- Languages used — confirms or expands JD tech stack

Extract: confirmed technologies, engineering culture signals, OSS philosophy.

### Engineering Blog / Tech Blog
Search: "[Company name] engineering blog"
- Topics they write about (reveals what they are proud of)
- Architecture posts (system design, scaling stories)
- Tooling choices (what they use and why)
- Author diversity (one person or whole team writing)

Extract: architecture patterns, technical challenges, engineering culture.

### Conference Talks
Search: "[Company name] engineering talk" or "[CTO name] talk"
- What problems they present publicly
- Technical depth and maturity signals

Extract: public technical challenges, thought leadership presence.

## Red Flag Indicators

Watch for these across all sources:
- **High turnover on LinkedIn** — many people with <1 year tenure
- **"Management" as a recurring Glassdoor con** — culture problem at top
- **Funding gap >2 years** — possible cash flow issues (for startups)
- **Mass layoffs within last 6 months** — role may be backfill into a demoralized team
- **Vague JD** — no specifics about team, stack, or problems = undefined role
- **CEO turnover** — new CEO within last year means strategy is in flux
- **Glassdoor rating below 3.0** — consistent pattern of problems
- **No engineering blog or OSS presence** (for a tech company) — engineering may not be valued

Red flags are not dealbreakers by default — they are topics to probe during the interview.

## Research Shortcuts by Company Type

### Healthcare Companies
Prioritize: HIPAA/HITRUST posture, EHR integrations, payer vs provider focus,
value-based care vs fee-for-service model, regulatory environment. These are
interview differentiators for Jimmy.

### Startups (Series A-C)
Prioritize: Runway (funding vs burn rate), founder background, product-market fit
signals, team size and growth rate, what the next 12 months look like.

### Enterprise / Public Companies
Prioritize: Recent earnings or strategic initiatives, org structure complexity,
which business unit this role serves, internal politics signals from Glassdoor.

### Former Employers
Skip general research — Jimmy already knows the company. Focus exclusively on:
- Leadership changes since departure
- New products or strategy pivots
- Headcount changes (growth or contraction)
- Recent news and market moves
- How the team/department has evolved
