# Company Research Output Templates

Templates for the three documents produced by the company-research skill.
Each template includes section headers and inline guidance on what quality looks like.

---

## Template 1: company-brief.md

The deep research document. Comprehensive — meant to be read once for understanding,
then referenced as needed. No length limit, but every section should earn its space.

```markdown
# Company Brief — [Company Name]

**Researched:** YYYY-MM-DD
**Role:** [Role Title]
**Sources:** [List each source consulted — e.g., company website, Glassdoor, Crunchbase]

## Company Overview
- **Founded:** [Year] | **HQ:** [City, State] | **Size:** [Headcount or range]
- **Industry:** [Vertical]
- **Stage:** [Startup / Growth / Enterprise / Public]
- **What they do:** [Plain-language description — not marketing copy]

## Leadership
| Name | Title | Background | Relevance |
|------|-------|------------|-----------|
| [CEO] | CEO/Founder | [Key background] | [Why it matters for the interview] |
| [CTO/VP Eng] | [Title] | [Key background] | [Likely interviewer — note their focus areas] |
| [Other] | [Title] | [Key background] | [If relevant to this role] |

## Product & Technology
- **Core product(s):** [What they build, who uses it]
- **Tech stack:** [Known technologies from JD, blog, GitHub]
- **Architecture:** [Microservices/monolith, cloud provider, notable patterns]
- **What they're building next:** [Signals from JD, job openings, news]

## Recent Moves (Last 6 Months)
| Date | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|-------------|
| [YYYY-MM] | [Funding / Launch / Hire / Acquisition] | [What it means for the company] |

## Culture & Work Environment
- **Glassdoor:** [Rating] — [Summary of consistent themes across 5+ reviews]
- **Work model:** [Remote / Hybrid / In-office]
- **Engineering culture:** [Signals from blog, OSS, reviews]
- **Interview process:** [What Glassdoor reviewers report about the process]

## Competitive Landscape
- **Direct competitors:** [Names and brief positioning]
- **Market trends:** [Relevant industry trends affecting this company]
- **Differentiator:** [What they claim sets them apart]

## What This Role Really Is
[Read between the lines of the JD. What problem are they hiring this person to solve?
Is this a builder, scaler, fixer, or maintainer role? What's the real urgency?]

## Connection Points to Your Background
| Their Need | Your Experience | Evidence |
|------------|----------------|----------|
| [Specific need from research] | [Achievement from achievements.md] | [Numbers] |
| [Second need] | [Achievement] | [Numbers] |
| [Third need] | [Achievement] | [Numbers] |
| [Gap area] | [Adjacent experience — honest bridge] | [How to frame it] |

## Red Flags & Unknowns
- [Anything concerning from research — or "None identified"]
- [Things that could not be verified]
- [Questions to ask during the interview to resolve unknowns]
```

## Template 2: study-sheet.md

The quick-reference memorization document. **Hard constraint: under 2 pages.**
If it cannot be reviewed in 10 minutes before the interview, it is too long.
Every word must earn its place.

```markdown
# Study Sheet — [Company Name] [Role]

**Review time: ~10 minutes**

## The Company in 30 Seconds
[2-3 sentences maximum. What they do, how big, what stage. Practice saying this aloud.]

## Key People
| Name | Title | Know This |
|------|-------|-----------|
| [CEO] | CEO | [One memorable fact] |
| [CTO/VP] | [Title] | [One fact — likely interviewer] |

## Their Big Moves (Last 6 Months)
1. [Most important recent event — one sentence]
2. [Second — one sentence]
3. [Third — one sentence]

## What They Need From This Hire
1. [Primary problem to solve]
2. [Secondary priority]
3. [Third priority]

## Your Top 3 Bridges
1. **[Their need]** → [Your story + number from achievements.md]
2. **[Their need]** → [Your story + number]
3. **[Their need]** → [Your story + number]

## Smart Things to Say
Phrases that demonstrate research without being rehearsed:
- "I noticed [specific recent event] — how has that affected [relevant area]?"
- "Your [product/approach] reminds me of [something from your experience]..."
- "The JD mentions [specific term] — at [your company], we approached that by..."

## Smart Things to Ask
1. [Question that references their recent move]
2. [Question about team/product that shows depth]
3. [Question about their technical approach]

## Numbers to Have Ready
| Your Metric | Number | Why It Matters to Them |
|-------------|--------|------------------------|
| [Achievement] | [Number] | [Company relevance] |
| [Achievement] | [Number] | [Company relevance] |
| [Achievement] | [Number] | [Company relevance] |
```

## Template 3: interview-plan.md

The strategic planning document. Bridges research to execution. This is about
**how to use what you learned**, not just what you learned.

```markdown
# Interview Plan — [Company Name] [Role]

**Strategy date:** YYYY-MM-DD

## Strategic Positioning
- **Primary angle:** [Which differentiator matters most to THIS company right now]
- **Secondary angle:** [Backup if the first doesn't resonate]
- **Avoid:** [Topics to steer away from based on research]

## Anticipated Interview Focus
Based on role type, company stage, and JD language:
- **They will care most about:** [Topic]
- **They will probably probe:** [Gap area from match_score]
- **Watch for questions about:** [Trending topic from research]

## Company-Specific Talking Points

### 1. [Topic tied to their biggest need]
**Your story:** [Achievement from achievements.md with numbers]
**Company connection:** [Why this matters specifically to THEM]
**Drop this when:** [Interview question or topic that triggers this story]

### 2. [Topic tied to their second need]
**Your story:** [Achievement with numbers]
**Company connection:** [Why this matters to them]
**Drop this when:** [Trigger]

### 3. [Topic tied to their third need]
**Your story:** [Achievement with numbers]
**Company connection:** [Why this matters to them]
**Drop this when:** [Trigger]

## Gap Strategy
For each gap from match_score, informed by company research:

### [Gap]
**Their likely concern:** [How important is this to them specifically?]
**Your bridge:** [Frame adjacent experience]
**If they push hard:** [Fallback response showing self-awareness]

## Questions That Show You Did Homework
1. "[References specific recent event and its impact]"
2. "[References their tech approach — shows you read the blog/GitHub]"
3. "[References team/org structure — shows you understand the role context]"

## Signals to Watch During the Interview
- Do they confirm or contradict [research finding]?
- How do they respond when you reference [company fact]?
- What do they NOT mention that research suggested is important?
```
