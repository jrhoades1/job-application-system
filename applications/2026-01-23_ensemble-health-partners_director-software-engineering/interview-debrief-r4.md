## Key Questions & Responses

### "How have you seen AI transform your work?"
- Deterministic foundation + AI enrichment on top
- Every AI decision logged and scored with human escalation path
- Pre-Thanksgiving 2025: "not ready yet" → Post-December 2025: "actually pretty good"
- Software deserts: $500K dev team for $1K problem — AI changes that economics
- Now arguing with AI about patterns/taste, not functionality

### "How have you overcome AI skepticism/resistance?"
- Guardrails first (especially in healthcare — HIPAA, HITRUST)
- Intent layers framework: values ("be kind to customer") → business rules ("keep turnover under 5%") → operational ("3-minute calls")
- Call center cautionary tale illustrating why intent hierarchy matters

### "Specific example of implementing AI / driving change?"
- Legacy platform at ilumed — team attached to it, revenue depended on it
- Incremental approach: maintain stability, add new capabilities alongside
- Let real data build the case — show call times dropping, quantify savings
- Earn trust through stability first, then innovate

### "How do you develop talent and upskill teams?"
- Three-phase approach: culture first → process second → scale third
- Culture: hire for collaborative fit, high standards, make the team better
- Process: agile ceremonies, CI/CD standards, non-negotiable quality gates, ownership of the problem (not just the task)
- Scale: becomes a hiring problem once culture and process are solid
- Cognizant/clearinghouse example: navigating "security team whose job was to say no" — building trust to move forward

### "Biggest mistake?"
- Engineer at heart — tendency to go too deep in technology
- "Chipmunk syndrome" — stuffing everything in at once, trying to solve all problems simultaneously
- Learned prioritization through experience
- Got MBA to bridge business/tech communication gap — can speak both languages

### "30-60-90 day plan?"
- **30 days:** Be quiet, be a sponge. Learn business ops (denial codes, payer quirks, workarounds), assess existing team and technology
- **60 days:** Start incremental changes based on what was learned, with guardrails
- **90 days:** Bigger changes and strategic initiatives
- **How:** Establish communication cadences (weekly/biweekly meetings), transparency, no games — "I want to go build something"

### "Cross-functional collaboration experience?"
- **Operations:** MedQuest — right patient on right equipment at right time, scheduling optimization ("drive 10 miles, get seen tomorrow")
- **Sales/marketing:** MedQuest — candy jar strategy for physician referrals → built routing/ranking app with Google Maps integration
- **Design:** Self-aware weakness ("black text white background looks good to me") — knows when to lean on design partners
- **Product:** Favorite part — "let's figure out what we really should be doing, not just be busy"
