Job Description
About the job

This employer will not sponsor applicants for employment visa status (e.g., H1-B) for this position.  All applicants must be currently authorized to work in the United States on a full-time basis.

Role Summary

The Director of Integration Engineering leads Loopback’s Data Integration team and serves as the senior-most authority on integration engineering. Reporting directly to the CTO, this role is accountable for the execution of integrations with a focus on speed, predictability, quality, and cost efficiency. The Director is a hands-on technical leader — reviewing code, mentoring engineers, and instilling engineering best practices — while driving outcomes at scale through automation, frameworks, and process discipline.

This role owns Loopback’s speed-to-value and unit economics for integrations, including both labor and infrastructure costs. The Director ensures new data sources are onboarded rapidly, accurately, and cost-effectively, with measurable impact on platform economics and client delivery outcomes.

Key Responsibilities

Integration Leadership: Lead and grow the Data Integration team (data analysts and engineers) with a focus on speed, quality, and predictability.
Technical Oversight: Provide hands-on review of Python, SQL, and Databricks code, ensuring technical excellence and maintainability.
Mentorship: Elevate team capability through structured coaching, code reviews, and setting engineering best practices.
Process Discipline: Define and enforce integration playbooks, onboarding frameworks, and delivery practices that reduce cycle times and defects.
Cross-Functional Partnership: Collaborate with Product, Data Engineering, RWD, and PMO to align integration outcomes with business priorities and client expectations.
Unit Economics & Cost Discipline: Own and track the economic performance of integrations, including cost-per-implementation, infrastructure costs, and opportunity cost of schedule delays — translating integration economics into actionable engineering decisions and strategies for leveraging labor alternatives.
Automation & Innovation: Drive initiatives that reduce manual effort in onboarding, optimize Snowflake/Databricks infrastructure spend, and improve speed-to-value.
Domain Expertise: Apply deep healthcare data knowledge (Epic, Cerner, pharmacy, claims, RWD) to accelerate mapping, normalization, and onboarding success.

Qualifications & Profile

Strong technical background in Python, SQL, Databricks, and/or Snowflake, with the ability to evaluate and guide code at depth.
Proven success leading integration or data engineering teams, with a record of mentoring and multiplying the impact of others.
Demonstrated success in healthcare data environments — Epic or Cerner integration experience strongly preferred.
Strong understanding of data onboarding, mapping, and normalization processes.
Experience in defining, tracking, and improving unit economics for technical delivery and infrastructure costs.
Effective cross-functional collaborator with Product, Data Engineering, PMO, and business stakeholders.
Balance of strategic mindset with willingness to “get hands dirty” in technical review and process definition.

Success Metrics

Cycle Time: Demonstrated reduction in onboarding duration, accelerating speed-to-value.
Quality: High first-pass acceptance of integrations with minimal rework required.
Predictability: Reliable delivery of integrations on committed schedules.
Automation & Frameworks: Increased leverage of automation and reusable patterns in integration delivery.
Cost Efficiency: Measurable reduction in both labor and infrastructure costs per integration.
Unit Economics: Defined, tracked, and continuously improved economics across integration types, incorporating cost-per-integration, opportunity cost of delay, and strategies for leveraging labor alternatives.
Client Impact: Measurable improvement in client confidence and satisfaction with Loopback’s delivery performance.
