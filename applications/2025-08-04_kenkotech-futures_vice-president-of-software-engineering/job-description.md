Job Description
About the job

🔶 Vice President of Software Engineering (Medical Imaging)

🔶 USA

🔶 Competitive Base, Bonus, Strong Equity

🔶 $150M + Funding, Strong Revenue

The client’s Engineering team powers an AI-driven, medical imaging platform focused on advancing heart disease detection and prevention. Operating in a regulated healthcare environment, the team emphasizes reliability, security, and performance.

They embrace lean and agile principles to enable rapid iteration, feedback-driven development, and a culture of continuous learning and innovation.

About the Opportunity:

The client is seeking a Vice President of Software Engineering to define and execute its technical vision, leading a world-class team to deliver scalable, high-quality software.

This role is ideal for a strategic technology leader who thrives in high-growth environments and wants to make a real impact in healthcare. You’ll align technology with business objectives, scale the engineering organization, and foster a culture of excellence and accountability.

Key Responsibilities

Build and Lead : Scale and manage a high-performing engineering team.
Champion Agile Practices : Drive lean and agile methodologies across teams.
Ensure Technical Excellence : Oversee software development, infrastructure, DevOps, and security.
Define Strategy : Collaborate with the CTO and senior leaders to execute the technology roadmap.
Support Compliance : Implement processes aligned with healthcare regulations.
Develop Leaders : Mentor and support engineering managers and technical leads.
Operational Excellence : Optimize processes to support growth and minimize waste.
Cross-Functional Collaboration : Align engineering priorities with Product, Clinical, and Commercial teams.
Metrics & Improvement : Establish KPIs to drive quality, reliability, and performance.

Requirements:

Proven experience scaling engineering teams in fast-paced, Agile environments.
Strong grasp of lean principles, cloud infrastructure (AWS, Azure, GCP), and full-stack development.
Experience in regulated industries such as healthcare or medical devices.
Excellent communication and collaboration skills.
Strategic problem-solver with a data-driven approach.
Comfortable with ambiguity and rapid growth.

Preferred Experience:

Background working in cardiology or cardiology-related technologies.
Familiarity with diagnostic imaging workflows and tools.
Exposure to clinical environments, EMRs, and PACS.
Experience building products in FDA-regulated settings.
